tips for choosing components
Processors are produced by two companies: Intel and AMD. We pay attention to the number of cores and threads, especially in game builds — modern games like multithreading. 6 cores and 12 threads are the minimum required for a gaming PC.
At the same price, the performance of competing CPUs will be approximately at the same level, but recently AMD processors have tended to become more expensive. First of all, look at the price and the availability of a built-in video core. For the assembly of a budget PC, this is important, as it allows you to save on the purchase of a video card.
Processor
Dozens of brands are engaged in the production of video cards, but only two companies produce chips for video cards — AMD and NVIDIA. Choose among the current generation of video cards, since the models of the last generation, although they may cost less, will become obsolete faster.
Be sure to look at the amount of video memory: the same models may differ in different volumes. Modern games like more memory. The noise level of the cooling system under load is important — two or three fans will remove heat more efficiently and make less noise.
Video card
The motherboard must be compatible with the previously selected processor, we look at it by the name of the socket and the chipset. Sometimes a manufacturer, using a single socket, changes the generation of the chipset, without ensuring compatibility with newly released processors. Pay attention to the number of slots for RAM — these are two or four DDR slots.
The more slots, the more flexible the platform is to increase the amount of RAM. It is also important to have different interfaces. For example, if you don't have an M.2 slot, you won't be able to install a high-speed drive in the future. However, even budget motherboards of current chipsets are equipped with most modern connectors.
Motherboard
The current standard of RAM today is DDR4, but there is a better memory — DDR5, it is more expensive, but faster. The supported format is indicated in the characteristics of the motherboard and processor.
The most important aspect is the volume and number of slats. The minimum for today is 8 GB, in game builds you will need 16 GB or more. Two slats allow you to use a two-channel operation mode. But, if there are only two slots, and you took two 4 GB slats, then in the future you will not be able to increase the volume without completely replacing the slats.
RAM memory
A drive is a traditional hard drive (HDD) or solid—state drive (SSD). The latter will be much faster in operation, but in terms of price/volume, it is still inferior to hard drives. Budget options for a 120 GB SSD are quite inexpensive, so as a system disk it is a mandatory element even for budget assemblies.
If you still need HDD as a data storage, then take it with a rotation speed of 7200 rpm and at least 1 TB, since the price of the 500 GB model differs slightly. For productive assemblies, an M.2 form factor drive is needed. But be sure to pay attention to the speed characteristics: budget models for reading/writing are no different from conventional SATA SSDs.
Drives
A reliable power supply unit will not only provide the components with a stable voltage, but also protect them in the event of a short circuit or an electrical network accident. An average computer with a single video card consumes about 500 watts, plus or minus 100 watts. Top gaming and professional builds can consume 1 kW.
The power should be chosen with a margin: add at least 20-30% to the peak consumption. You also need to focus on certification. Power supplies with the 80Plus certificate are better in efficiency and, as a rule, are equipped with more reliable components and are built on efficient circuitry. It is also worth considering the number of connectors for connecting peripherals and the length of the wires.
Power unit
The selection of the cooling system, if it is not included with the CPU, is carried out according to the heat dissipation characteristic (TDP) — we look at the TDP of the processor and the same indicator for the cooler. It is better to choose at least with a one-and-a-half margin. Among the cooling systems can be distinguished: conventional horizontal coolers, tower coolers with heat pipes and liquid cooling systems.
It is important that the selected cooling system fits the motherboard socket: there are universal mounts included, but cheaper options can support only one type of socket mount.
CPU Cooling
The selected cooler must fit into the housing. Not only the appearance of the computer depends on the case, but also the "comfort" of all components — the temperature regime is important for them. The case should be well blown, it is desirable that it is equipped with at least two fans — for blowing and blowing, otherwise they will have to be bought separately.
Modern enclosures support the installation of power supplies from below — this is the most profitable option for its comfortable operation. There should be a sufficient number of interfaces on the front side, including high-speed USB 3.2 ports, as well as universal Type-C.
